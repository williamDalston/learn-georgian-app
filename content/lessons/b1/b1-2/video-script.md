# Lesson B1-2: Preverbs: Directionality and Perfective Aspect

**Level:** B1
**Duration:** 65 minutes
**Module:** 3 | **Lesson:** 2
**Estimated Hours:** 1.08 hours

## Learning Objectives
By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:
- Understand what preverbs are and their dual function in Georgian
- Use preverbs to express directionality and perfective aspect
- Master the most common preverbs (მი-, აკ-, ჩაკ-, გა-, etc.)
- Recognize when to use preverbs vs base verb forms
- Construct sentences with directional and aspectual meaning
- Identify perfective vs imperfective aspect in Georgian

## Key Concepts
- Preverbs (prefixes that modify verbs)
- Directionality (where the action moves)
- Perfective aspect (completed action)
- Imperfective aspect (ongoing/repeated action)
- Preverb → Present/Future = Perfective
- No Preverb → Present/Future = Imperfective

### Introduction (0:30 - 5:00)
**[Show comparison: same verb with and without preverb]**

"Look at these two Georgian sentences:
1. ვწერ წიგნს [vts'er ts'igns] - 'I am writing a book'
2. დავწერ წიგნს [davts'er ts'igns] - 'I will write a book'

Same verb root 'write,' but completely different meaning! What's the difference? The preverb დაუ- [dau-] in the second sentence!

**[Display visual explanation]**

Preverbs are prefixes added to verbs that do TWO things at once:
1. **Show direction** - where the action moves in space
2. **Show if action is complete** - whether the action is finished or still happening

Let me show you with a concrete example:

**[Show movement demonstration]**

The verb ხედვა [khedva] means 'to see' (no movement).
Add the preverb მი- [mi-] and we get მიხედვა [mikhedva] - 'to look toward' or 'to direct one's gaze.'

**[Display sentences]**

ვხედავ კაცს [vkhedav k'ats's] - 'I see a man' (just perceiving)
ვუყურებ კაცს [vuqu'reb k'ats's] - 'I look at/watch a man' (directing attention)
მივხედავ კაცს [mivkhedav k'ats's] - 'I will look at/look toward the man' (perfective)

Do you see the pattern? Preverbs add nuance and completion!"

### Main Content Section 1: Understanding Aspect (5:00 - 20:00)
**[Display aspect timeline]**

"First, let's understand aspect. Aspect answers the question: Is the action complete or ongoing?

**Imperfective Aspect:** 
- Ongoing action
- Repeated action
- Habitual action
- Examples: 'I am writing,' 'I write daily,' 'I keep writing'

**Perfective (Completed Action):**
- The action is finished or will be finished
- A single, complete event
- Examples: 'I wrote (finished),' 'I will write (and complete it),' 'I have written'

**[Display Georgian comparison]**

In Georgian, the presence or absence of a preverb determines aspect!

**Without preverb (Ongoing Action):**
- ვწერ წიგნს [vts'er ts'igns] - 'I am writing the book' (ongoing)
- ვმუშაობ [vmushaob] - 'I work/I am working' (ongoing habit)

**With preverb (Completed Action):**
- დავწერ წიგნს [davts'er ts'igns] - 'I will write the book' (and complete it)
- მოვიმუშავებ [movimushaveb] - 'I will work (for a period and finish)'

**[Show conjugation table]**

Let's look at a complete conjugation. Here's წერა [ts'era] 'to write':

**Imperfective (no preverb):**
- მე ვწერ წიგნს [me vts'er ts'igns] - I am writing the book
- შენ წერ წიგნს [shen ts'er ts'igns] - You are writing the book
- ის წერს წიგნს [is ts'ers ts'igns] - He/She is writing the book

**Perfective (with preverb):**
- მე დავწერ წიგნს [me davts'er ts'igns] - I will write the book
- შენ დაწერ წიგნს [shen dats'er ts'igns] - You will write the book
- ის დაწერს წიგნს [is dats'ers ts'igns] - He/She will write the book

**[Highlight the difference]**

Notice: Same verb, same Series I, but completely different aspect!
- Without preverb = ongoing/imperfective
- With preverb = completed/perfective"

### Main Content Section 2: Directionality (20:00 - 38:00)
**[Display directional diagram]**

"Now for the second function: directionality. Preverbs indicate WHERE the action moves!

Think of yourself as a central point. Actions can move:
- **Toward you** (შენ - შენთან [shen - shent'an])
- **Away from you** (შენ - შენგან [shen - sheng'an])
- **Up** (ზევით [zevit])
- **Down** (ქვემოთ [kvemot])
- **Into** something (შიგნით [shig'nit])
- **Out of** something (გარეთ [garet])

**[Show preverb chart]**

Here are the main preverbs and their direction meanings:

**მი- [mi-]** - Toward
- მიხედვა [mikhedva] - look toward
- მიწერა [mits'era] - write toward/send
- მიშენება [misheneba] - attach to

**გა- [ga-]** - Out, Away
- გამიხედვა [gamikhedva] - look out
- გამოწერა [gamots'era] - copy out, transcribe
- განაწერა [ganats'era] - distinguish

**აკ- [ak'-]** - Up
- აკრეფა [ak'rep'a] - pick up
- აკვლა [ak'vla] - go up

**ჩაკ- [ch'ak'-]** - Into
- ჩაკრება [ch'ak'reba] - enter
- ჩაწერა [ch'ats'era] - write into, record

**და- [da-]** - Down, Together, Perfective marker
- დაკვლა [dak'vla] - go down
- დასკვნა [dask'vna] - draw conclusion (mind going down to conclusion)

**შე- [she-]** - In
- შეკრება [shek'reba] - gather in

**წა- [ts'a-]** - Up, Away
- წაკვლა [ts'ak'vla] - go up

**[Interactive exercise]**

Let's practice identifying directionality:

Example 1: ის ბაღში მიდის [is baghshi midis] - 'He goes toward the garden'
- Preverb? მი- [mi-] 
- Direction? Toward (the garden)

Example 2: ბავშვები გარეთ გამოვიდნენ [bavshvebi garet gamovidnen] - 'The children went out'
- Preverb? გა- [ga-] / გამო- [gamo-]
- Direction? Out (გარეთ [garet] = outside)

Example 3: ჩვენ მაღლა ავედით [ch'ven maghla avedit] - 'We went up'
- Preverb? აკ- [ak'-]
- Direction? Up (მაღლა [maghla] = upward)"

### Main Content Section 3: Common Preverbs in Detail (38:00 - 55:00)
**[Deep dive into most common preverbs]**

"Let's study the most frequently used preverbs with real examples:

**მი- [mi-] - Toward**
This is one of the most common preverbs.

Base: ხედვა [khedva] 'see' → Preverb: მიხედვა [mikhedva] 'look toward'
- ვუყურებ ფანჯარიდან [vuqu'reb panjaridan] - I am looking out the window (ongoing)
- მივხედავ ფანჯარიდან [mivkhedav panjaridan] - I will look out the window (complete action)

**[Show more examples]**

მე - მიხედვა [me - mikhedva]:
- მე მივხედავ შენკენ [me mivkhedav shenken] - I will look toward you

**[Continue with other preverbs]**

**გა- [ga-] - Out, Away**
Base: წერა [ts'era] 'write' → Preverb: გამოწერა [gamots'era] 'copy out'

Examples:
- დავწერ წიგნს [davts'er ts'igns] - I will write the book (complete)
- გამოვწერ ციტატას [gamovts'er ts'it'at'as] - I will copy out the quote

**[Display chart]**

**ჩაკ- [ch'ak'-] - Into**
Base: ჩაწერა [ch'ats'era] 'record, write into'

Example:
- ჩავწერ [ch'avts'er] - I will record it
- ჩასვლა [chasvla] - entering, going into

**[Show contrast sentences]**

**შე- [she-] - In**
Base: შეკრება [shek'reba] 'gather'

Examples:
- შევკრიბები ფული [shevk'rebebi puli] - I will gather money
- შევიდა ოთახში [shevida otakhshi] - He entered the room

**[Show combined examples]**

Let's see how preverbs combine with different verb roots:

მივიღე წერილი [mivigh'e ts'erili] - I received a letter (mi- toward me)
გამოვიღე წერილი [gamovigh'e ts'erili] - I sent a letter (ga- away from me)

Same verb root იღებს [ighebs] 'take/receive,' but opposite directions!"

### Summary (58:00 - 62:00)
**[Review key points]**

"Let's review what we learned:

1. **Preverbs** are prefixes that modify verbs
2. **Two functions**: Directionality + Aspect
3. **No preverb** = Imperfective (ongoing/repeated action)
4. **With preverb** = Perfective (completed action)
5. **Common preverbs**:
   - მი- [mi-] - toward
   - გა- [ga-] - out, away
   - აკ- [ak'-] - up
   - ჩაკ- [ch'ak'-] - into
   - და- [da-] - down, perfective
   - შე- [she-] - in

**[Display challenge sentence]**

Here's a challenge: Analyze this sentence:
- ის მივიდა ოთახში და დაჯდა [is mivida otakhshi da dadjda]
  - He went into the room and sat down

Can you identify both preverbs?
- მი- [mi-] in მივიდა [mivida] - went TOWARD/into
- და- [da-] in დაჯდა [dadjda] - sat DOWN

You're mastering this!"
