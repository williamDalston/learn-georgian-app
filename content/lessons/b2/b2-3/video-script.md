# Lesson B2-3: Ergative Case Marking in Past Transitive Verbs

**Level:** B2
**Duration:** 70 minutes
**Module:** 4 | **Lesson:** 3
**Estimated Hours:** 1.17 hours

## Learning Objectives
By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:
- Identify when to use Ergative case for transitive subjects
- Recognize Ergative case markers (-მა suffix and other forms)
- Distinguish between Ergative and Nominative subjects based on verb class and tense
- Apply Ergative marking correctly in Series II and III
- Understand exceptions and special cases

## Key Concepts
- Ergative case
- Transitive verbs
- Past tense (Series II/III)
- Case marking rules
- Verb classes

### Introduction: When Do We Use Ergative? (0:45 - 10:00)
**[Show decision tree: Ergative vs Nominative]**

"Let's start with the golden rule: **Ergative case is used for transitive subjects in Series II (Aorist) and Series III (Perfect).**

**Key Rule:**
- **Series I (Present/Future):** Transitive subjects = Nominative
- **Series II (Aorist):** Transitive subjects = Ergative
- **Series III (Perfect):** Transitive subjects = Ergative
- **Intransitive verbs:** Subjects always stay Nominative (all series)

**[Show examples]**
**Series I - Nominative:**
მე ვხედავ კატას [me vkhedav kat'as] - 'I see the cat'
- მე [me] = Nominative (subject of transitive verb in present)

**Series II - Ergative:**
მე ვნახე კატა [me vnakhe kat'a] - 'I saw the cat'
- მე [me] = Ergative (subject of transitive verb in past)
- Note: Personal pronouns like მე often look the same, but are Ergative in function

**Intransitive - Always Nominative:**
მე ვირბინე [me virbine] - 'I ran'
- მე [me] = Nominative (subject of intransitive verb, all series)

So the pattern is clear: **Transitive + Past/Perfect = Ergative**"

---

### Main Content Section 1: Ergative Case Markers (10:00 - 35:00)
**[Show Ergative suffixes and forms]**

"Now let's learn how Ergative case is actually marked. Different types of nouns and pronouns have different Ergative markers.

**1. Personal Pronouns:**

First and second person pronouns often look the same in Ergative, but third person changes:

**[Show pronoun table]**
- მე [me] 'I' → მე [me] (same form, Ergative function)
- შენ [shen] 'you' → შენ [shen] (same form, Ergative function)
- ის [is] 'he/she' → მან [man] (different form, Ergative)
- ჩვენ [chven] 'we' → ჩვენ [chven] (same form, Ergative function)
- თქვენ [tkven] 'you (pl)' → თქვენ [tkven] (same form, Ergative function)
- ისინი [isini] 'they' → მათ [mat] (different form, Ergative)

**Key Point:** Third person pronouns change form (ის → მან, ისინი → მათ)

**2. Common Nouns with -მა Suffix:**

Most common nouns add -მა [ma] for Ergative case:

**[Show examples]**
- ბავშვი [bavshvi] 'child' → ბავშვმა [bavshvma] (Ergative)
- მასწავლებელი [masts'avlebeli] 'teacher' → მასწავლებელმა [masts'avlebelma] (Ergative)
- კატა [kat'a] 'cat' → კატამა [kat'ama] (Ergative)
- სტუდენტი [st'udent'i] 'student' → სტუდენტმა [st'udent'ma] (Ergative)

**Pattern:** Most nouns ending in -ი drop it and add -მა
- ბავშვ-ი → ბავშვ-მა
- მასწავლებელ-ი → მასწავლებელ-მა

**3. Nouns Ending in Vowels Other Than -ი:**

**[Show examples]**
- სახლი [sakhli] 'house' → სახლმა [sakhlma] (if transitive subject, but houses don't usually act!)
- ბავშვი → ბავშვმა (we saw this)

**4. Special Cases:**

Some nouns have irregular Ergative forms. The best way is to learn them as you encounter them, but here are patterns:

- Nouns ending in -ა [a]: კატა [kat'a] → კატამა [kat'ama]
- Some nouns may use -თ [t] in certain contexts
- Plural nouns: სტუდენტები [st'udent'ebi] 'students' → სტუდენტებმა [st'udent'ebma]

**Practice Recognition:**
Let's identify Ergative forms:
- ბავშვმა [bavshvma] → Ergative of 'child'
- მასწავლებელმა [masts'avlebelma] → Ergative of 'teacher'
- მან [man] → Ergative of 'he/she'"

### Main Content Section 2: Recognizing Transitive vs Intransitive (35:00 - 50:00)
**[Show verb classification]**

"Remember: **Only transitive verbs trigger Ergative marking.** So it's crucial to identify whether a verb is transitive or intransitive.

**How to Identify Transitive Verbs:**
- They take a direct object
- They answer "what?" or "whom?"
- Examples: 'see', 'read', 'write', 'teach', 'love'

**How to Identify Intransitive Verbs:**
- They don't take a direct object
- They describe actions without affecting something else
- Examples: 'run', 'sleep', 'go', 'come', 'arrive'

**[Show comparison]**
**Transitive (takes Ergative in past):**
- მან წაიკითხა წიგნი [man ts'aikit'kha ts'igni] - 'He read the book'
  - Subject: მან (Ergative) because verb is transitive

**Intransitive (stays Nominative):**
- ის წავიდა [is ts'avida] - 'He went'
  - Subject: ის (Nominative) because verb is intransitive

**[Show tricky cases]**
Some verbs can be both transitive and intransitive:

'to read':
- Transitive: მან წაიკითხა წიგნი [man ts'aikit'kha ts'igni] - 'He read the book' (Ergative)
- Intransitive: ის კითხულობს [is kit'khulobs] - 'He reads' (Nominative, no object)

The presence of an object determines transitivity!"

### Summary (63:00 - 68:00)
**[Review key points]**

"Let's review Ergative case marking:

1. **When to Use Ergative:**
   - Transitive verbs in Series II (Aorist)
   - Transitive verbs in Series III (Perfect)
   - NOT for Series I (Present/Future) - those use Nominative
   - NOT for intransitive verbs - those always use Nominative

2. **How to Mark Ergative:**
   - Most common nouns: add -მა suffix (ბავშვი → ბავშვმა)
   - Third person pronouns: change form (ის → მან, ისინი → მათ)
   - First/second person pronouns: same form, different function (მე, შენ)

3. **Identifying Transitivity:**
   - Transitive = takes direct object
   - Intransitive = no direct object
   - Same verb can be both depending on usage

4. **The Formula:**
   Transitive + Series II/III = Ergative subject
   Intransitive + Any Series = Nominative subject

Master this, and you'll mark Ergative correctly every time!"
