# Lesson A1-5: Basic Greetings and Survival Phrases

**Level:** A1
**Duration:** 30 minutes
**Module:** 1 | **Lesson:** 5
**Estimated Hours:** 0.5 hours

## Learning Objectives
By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:
- Greet people formally and informally in Georgian
- Express gratitude and basic courtesy
- Introduce yourself using the formal address system
- Use essential survival phrases for daily interactions

## Key Concepts
- Greetings (gamarjoba, nakhvamdis)
- Formal address (Batono/Kalbatono)
- Expressions of gratitude
- Survival phrases

**[Warm greeting in Georgian]**

"Gamarjoba! I'm so glad you're here. Today we're learning something incredibly important - how to greet people and use basic phrases in Georgian. These are the words you'll use every single day, so let's master them!"

### Introduction (0:30 - 2:00)

"In Georgia, greetings and formal address are deeply rooted in respect and tradition. Unlike many languages, Georgian has a specific formal address system that's essential for showing respect. ### Main Content Section 1: Basic Greetings (2:00 - 12:00)

**Gamarjoba (გამარჯობა) - Hello**

"Let's start with the most important word: გამარჯობა [gamarjoba] - Hello.

**[Show pronunciation breakdown: ga-mar-jo-ba]**

This word can be used:
- Any time of day
- For both formal and informal situations
- When meeting someone or answering the phone

**[Show examples]**
- გამარჯობა! - Hello! (informal or formal)
- გამარჯობა, როგორ ხართ? - Hello, how are you? (formal)

**Nakhvamdis (ნახვამდის) - Goodbye**

"Now for goodbye: ნახვამდის [nakhvamdis] - Goodbye.

**[Show pronunciation: nakh-vam-dis]**

This literally means 'until we see (each other)'. It's polite and works in all situations.

**[Show examples]**
- ნახვამდის! - Goodbye!
- ნახვამდის, გმადლობთ! - Goodbye, thank you!

**Other Greetings:**
- დილა მშვიდობისა [dila mshvidobisa] - Good morning
- საღამო მშვიდობისა [saghamo mshvidobisa] - Good evening
- ღამე მშვიდობისა [ghame mshvidobisa] - Good night"

---

### Main Content Section 2: Expressing Gratitude (12:00 - 18:00)

**Didi Madloba (დიდი მადლობა) - Thank You Very Much**

"Georgians value gratitude. Here are the ways to say thank you:

- მადლობა [madloba] - Thank you
- დიდი მადლობა [didi madloba] - Thank you very much / Big thanks

**[Show pronunciation and usage]**

You can respond with:
- არაფერს [arap'ers] - You're welcome / It's nothing
- გთხოვთ [gt'khovt'] - Please / You're welcome

**[Practice with scenarios]**

**[Show interaction examples]**
- A: მადლობა - Thank you
- B: არაფერს - You're welcome"

---

### Main Content Section 3: Formal Address System (18:00 - 25:00)

**Batono / Kalbatono (ბატონო / ქალბატონო)**

"This is crucial! Georgian uses formal titles:

- ბატონო [batono] - Sir / Mr.
- ქალბატონო [kalbatono] - Madam / Mrs. / Ms.

**[Show proper usage]**

When to use formal address:
- Speaking to older people
- Professional settings
- First meeting someone
- Showing respect

**Introducing Yourself Formally:**

**[Show example]**
გამარჯობა, ბატონო. ჩემი სახელია [Your Name]. - Hello, sir. My name is [Your Name].

გამარჯობა, ქალბატონო. მე ვარ [Your Name]. - Hello, madam. I am [Your Name]."

### Summary (28:00 - 29:30)
