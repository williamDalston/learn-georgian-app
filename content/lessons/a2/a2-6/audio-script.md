## Section 1: Formal Address for Men (0:45 - 4:00)

### ბატონო [batono] - Sir, Mr.

"ბატონო - ბატონო - ბატონო [bɑtʼɔnɔ]
ბატონო - sir, Mr.

**[Repeat after me]**
ბატონო - ბატონო - ბატონო

**[Pronunciation breakdown]**
BA-to-no

**[Pronunciation tips]**
- Formal address for men
- Contains ejective ტ [tʼ] sound
- Stress on first syllable: BA-to-no
- Used with strangers, elders, in professional settings

**[Practice in context - Greeting]**
გამარჯობა, ბატონო
gamarjoba, batono
Hello, sir

**[Repeat after me]**
გამარჯობა, ბატონო

**[Cultural note]**
This is respectful and appropriate when addressing men you don't know well, especially older men or in professional contexts.

**[Practice in context - In sentence]**
ბატონო, როგორ ხართ?
batono, rogor khart?
Sir, how are you?

**[Repeat after me]**
ბატონო, როგორ ხართ?"

## Section 3: Comparison and Context (7:30 - 10:00)

### When to Use Formal Address

"**Formal address (ბატონო / ქალბატონო) is used:**
- With strangers
- With elders
- In professional settings
- In formal situations
- When showing respect

**Informal address is used:**
- With friends
- With family
- With peers your age
- In casual settings

**[Practice comparison]**

**Formal:**
გამარჯობა, ბატონო
gamarjoba, batono
Hello, sir

**[Repeat after me]**
გამარჯობა, ბატონო

**Informal:**
გამარჯობა
gamarjoba
Hello

**[Repeat after me]**
გამარჯობა

Notice the difference? Formal address shows respect and cultural awareness!"
