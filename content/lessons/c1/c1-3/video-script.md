### Introduction: Literal vs. Cultural Language (0:50 - 5:00)
**[Show comparison examples]**

"First, let's understand what we mean by idioms. An idiom is a phrase whose meaning can't be understood from the individual words. 

For example, in English, if someone is 'under the weather,' you don't literally look for weather above them - it means they're sick!

Georgian is rich with idioms that reflect the culture, history, and way of thinking. Many of these idioms are poetic, beautiful, and deeply rooted in Georgian traditions.

Let me show you why this matters:

**Literal Translation (WRONG):**
- 'სახლს მიდის' literally = 'goes to house'
- What it ACTUALLY means: 'goes home'

**Idiomatic Expression:**
- 'ბედნიერ ვარსკვლავზე ბოლოვნება' = 'born on a happy star'
- What it ACTUALLY means: 'lucky / fortunate person'

See the difference? Idioms give you the real Georgian way of expressing ideas. Let's start learning some essential ones..."

---

### Main Content Section 1: Positive Idioms (5:00 - 20:00)
**[Show idiom with illustration]**

"Let's begin with positive expressions - things you'll want to say about good people and situations:

**Idiom 1: ბედნიერ ვარსკვლავზე ბოლოვნება / bednier varsk'vlavze bolovneba**

**Literal Translation:** Born on a happy star  
**Actual Meaning:** To be very lucky or fortunate  
**Usage:** This is one of the most beautiful Georgian idioms. It refers to someone blessed with good fortune.

**[Show example sentences]**

**Example:**
- ის ბედნიერ ვარსკვლავზე ბოლოვნებაა - მან ლატარეა მოიგო!
- is bednier varsk'vlavze bolovnebaa - man lat'area moigo!
- 'He/She is born on a happy star - he/she won the lottery!'

We use this to express admiration for someone's good luck in life.

**Idiom 2: გული აქვს ბრძოლისათვის / guli aqvs brdzolisat'vis**

**Literal Translation:** Has heart for battle  
**Actual Meaning:** Courageous, brave, determined  
**Usage:** Someone with great inner strength and determination.

**Example:**
- ჩემი მეგობარი გული აქვს ბრძოლისათვის - ყოველთვის იბრძვის.
- chemi megobari guli aqvs brdzolisat'vis - qovelt'vis ibrdzvis.
- 'My friend has heart for battle - he/she always fights/struggles.'

This reflects Georgian warrior culture and values of courage.

**Idiom 3: ხელი აქვს / kheli aqvs**

**Literal Translation:** Has hand  
**Actual Meaning:** Skilled, talented, has ability  
**Usage:** Someone with natural talent or skill.

**Example:**
- ეს ხელოვნები ხელი აქვს მუსიკისათვის.
- es khelovnebi kheli aqvs musik'isat'vis.
- 'These artists have hand for music' = 'These artists are talented at music.'

Georgians have many expressions using 'ხელი' (hand) to describe skill.

Let's learn a few more..."

### Main Content Section 2: Emotional/Descriptive Idioms (20:00 - 38:00)
**[Show emotion idioms]**

"Now let's explore idioms that describe emotions and states:

**Idiom 4: ყურები ჩამოეცვა / q'urebi chamoets'va**

**Literal Translation:** Ears dropped  
**Actual Meaning:** Sad, disappointed, dejected  
**Usage:** When someone's mood visibly drops.

**Example:**
- როდესაც გამოცდას ვერ ჩავაბარე, ყურები ჩამოეცვა.
- rodesac gamotsdas ver chavabare, q'urebi chamoets'va.
- 'When I failed the exam, my ears dropped' = 'I became very sad.'

This creates a vivid image - someone slumping with disappointment.

**Idiom 5: ვირიან თავზე დაჯდომა / virian tavze dajdoma**

**Literal Translation:** Sitting on a donkey's head  
**Actual Meaning:** Stubborn, willfully obstinate  
**Usage:** Someone who refuses to change their mind.

**Example:**
- ჩემი მეგობარი ვირიან თავზე დაჯდომაა - არ დავტოვებს თავის დამოკიდებულებას.
- chemi megobari virian tavze dajdomaa - ar davt'oebs tavs damok'idebulebas.
- 'My friend is sitting on a donkey's head - he/she won't give up their position.'

This humorous image perfectly captures stubbornness!

**Idiom 6: ცეცხლი გადაენაცვლება / tsetskhli gadaenatsvleba**

**Literal Translation:** Fire is exchanged  
**Actual Meaning:** Very energetic, lively, dynamic  
**Usage:** Someone or something full of energy.

**Example:**
- ამ ბავშვებს ცეცხლი გადაენაცვლება!
- am bavshevs tsetskhli gadaenatsvleba!
- 'These children have fire exchanged!' = 'These children are extremely energetic.'

This idiom literally describes exchanging fire, meaning incredible vitality.

**Idiom 7: წვერი აქვს / ts'veri aqvs**

**Literal Translation:** Has beard  
**Actual Meaning:** Experienced, wise, mature  
**Usage:** Respectful way to say someone is wise or experienced.

**Example:**
- ჩემი დედა წვერი აქვს - იცის ყველაფერი.
- chemi deda ts'veri aqvs - its'is q'velaperi.
- 'My mother has beard' = 'My mother is wise and experienced.'

This is a respectful idiom for wisdom - though women don't literally have beards!"

### Main Content Section 3: Negative/Cautionary Idioms (43:00 - 55:00)
**[Show warning idioms]**

"Georgian also has idioms for difficult or negative situations:

**Idiom 8: დაწყნარების ნისლი / dats'q'narebis nisli**

**Literal Translation:** Fog of confusion  
**Actual Meaning:** State of being confused, unclear  
**Usage:** When things are not clear or confusing.

**Example:**
- ეს თემა დაწყნარების ნისლში არის.
- es tema dats'q'narebis nisli-ashi aris.
- 'This topic is in a fog of confusion' = 'This topic is very unclear/confusing.'

**Idiom 9: ყველაფერი წყლში ფლობს / q'velaperi ts'qlshi plobs**

**Literal Translation:** Everything floats in water  
**Actual Meaning:** Everything is uncertain, unstable  
**Usage:** Describing an unstable or uncertain situation.

**Example:**
- ჩვენი გეგმები ყველაფერი წყლში ფლობს.
- chveni gegnebi q'velaperi ts'qlshi plobs.
- 'Our plans, everything floats in water' = 'Our plans are very uncertain.'

**Idiom 10: საზღვარი არ ვიცი / sazghvari ar vitsi**

**Literal Translation:** I don't know the border  
**Actual Meaning:** I don't know when to stop / I'm excessive  
**Usage:** Admitting one goes too far or doesn't know limits.

**Example:**
- საკვებში საზღვარი არ ვიცი - ვჭამ ბევრს.
- sak'vebshi sazghvari ar vitsi - vch'am bevrs.
- 'I don't know the border in food - I eat a lot.'

This is a humble self-admission of excess."
