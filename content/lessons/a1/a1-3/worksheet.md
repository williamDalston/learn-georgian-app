## Answer Key

**Part 1:**
კ / [kʼ] / Ejective k
პ / [pʼ] / Ejective p
ტ / [tʼ] / Ejective t
წ / [tsʼ] / Ejective ts
ჭ / [tʃʼ] / Ejective ch
ყ / [qʼ] / Back velar ejective

**Part 2:**
1. Close your throat (vocal cords)
2. Build pressure in the mouth cavity
3. Release the closure with a pop

**Part 3:**
1. A (aspirated) 2. E (ejective) 3. A (aspirated) 4. E (ejective) 5. A (aspirated) 6. E (ejective)

**Part 4:**
1. Initial (კ) 2. Initial and Medial (კ, ტ) 3. Initial (ტ) 4. Initial and Medial (both პ)

**Part 5:**
1. 2 ejectives: წ [tsʼ] and ყ [qʼ]
2. 2 ejectives: both პ [pʼ]
3. 2 ejectives: კ [kʼ] and ტ [tʼ]
4. 2 ejectives: ტ [tʼ] and ყ [qʼ]

**Part 6:**
1. კ (კაცი) 2. პ (პაპა) 3. ტ (ტბა) 4. წ (წყალი) 5. კ (კაბა) 6. ყ (ყვავილი) 7. წ (წიგნი) 8. ჭ (ჭა)

**Part 7:**
1. ყ [qʼ] because it's made way back in the throat, almost at the uvula
2. Aspirated has air puff, ejective has throat pop
3. Pronunciation habits fossilize - hard to change later

**Part 8:**
1. კ, ტ
2. წ (appears twice)
3. პ (appears twice), კ, ტ
