### Introduction: From Simple to Complex (0:45 - 4:00)
**[Show sentence complexity chart]**

"What makes a sentence complex? It's when we embed one clause inside another or link multiple clauses together. Georgian has several powerful strategies for this:

**Three Main Types of Embedding:**
1. **Complement Clauses** - When a clause functions as the object or subject of a verb
2. **Adverbial Clauses** - When a clause modifies the verb, answering questions like 'when,' 'why,' 'how,' or 'if'
3. **Relative Clauses** - Which we covered in lesson one

**Two Main Linking Strategies:**
1. **Coordination** - Linking equal clauses with conjunctions like 'and,' 'but,' 'or'
2. **Subordination** - Making one clause dependent on another

Today, we'll master all of these patterns. Let's start with complement clauses - probably the most frequent type in natural Georgian."

---

### Main Content Section 1: Complement Clauses (4:00 - 28:00)
**[Show complement clause structure diagram]**

"Complement clauses are clauses that function as the object or subject of a verb. Think of verbs like 'to think,' 'to say,' 'to know,' 'to believe' - they need something to complement their meaning.

In Georgian, we typically use რომ (rom) for complement clauses. Let me show you the pattern:

**Basic Pattern:** [Main Verb] რომ [Complement Clause]

**[Show examples on screen]**

**Example 1 - Saying/Telling:**
- მე ვფიქრობ რომ ქართული ენა ძალიან საინტერესოა.
- me vp'ik'rob rom kartuli ena dzalian saint'eresoa.
- 'I think that the Georgian language is very interesting.'

Notice: ვფიქრობ (I think) + რომ + embedded clause.

**Example 2 - Knowing:**
- ის იცის რომ ჩვენ ჩვენი საქმე ვაკეთებთ.
- is its'is rom chven chveni sakme vak'etebt.
- 'He/she knows that we are doing our work.'

**Example 3 - With Different Verbs:**
Let's look at more verbs that take complement clauses:

**[Show verb list]**

**Common Verbs Taking Complement Clauses:**
- ვფიქრობ (vp'ik'rob) - 'I think'
- ვიცი (vitsi) - 'I know'
- ვთქვი (vtqvi) - 'I said'
- ვგრძნობ (vgrtsnob) - 'I feel/sense'
- ვამბობ (vambob) - 'I tell'
- ვიმედოვნებ (vimedovneb) - 'I hope'
- ვფიქრობდი (vp'ik'robdi) - 'I thought/was thinking'

**More Examples:**

Example 4:
- ოჯახი ვამბობ რომ სუპრაზე მოვიდე.
- ojakh'i vambob rom supr'eze movide.
- 'I'm telling the family that I should come to the supra.'

Example 5:
- მე ვიმედოვნებ რომ გამოცდაში ჩავაბარებ.
- me vimedovneb rom gamotsdashi chavabareb.
- 'I hope that I will pass the exam.'

**Important:** Notice how the embedded verb is in its full form - the complementizer რომ takes the full verb, not the participle form we saw in relative clauses.

Let's practice identifying complement clauses..."

### Practice Exercise 1 (28:00 - 34:00)
**[Interactive sentence analysis]**

"Let's break down a very complex sentence together:

**[Show sentence on screen]**
ის მიყვა რომ იცოდა რომ ჩემი მეგობარი დაწერს წიგნს რომელიც ძალიან პოპულარული გახდება.
is mik'va rom its'oda rom chemi megobari dats'ers ts'igns romel-i dzalian p'op'ularuli gadakhdeba.

Let's identify all the clauses:
1. მიყვა (main verb - 'said/told me')
2. რომ იცოდა... (complement clause 1 - 'that he/she knew')
3. რომ ჩემი მეგობარი დაწერს... (complement clause 2, embedded in clause 1 - 'that my friend will write')
4. რომელიც ძალიან პოპულარული გახდება (relative clause modifying წიგნს)

Translation: 'He/She told me that he/she knew that my friend would write a book which would become very popular.'

This is a triple embedding: complement inside complement inside complement! This is exactly the kind of sophistication you need at C1 level."

### Main Content Section 2: Adverbial Clauses (34:00 - 50:00)
**[Show adverbial clause types chart]**

"Adverbial clauses modify verbs, telling us when, where, why, how, or under what conditions something happens. Georgian has several conjunctions for these:

**Temporal Clauses (When):**

**[Show temporal conjunctions]**

- როდესაც (rodesac) - 'when'
- სანამ (sanam) - 'until / while'
- ასე რომ (ase rom) - 'so that'

Example 1:
- როდესაც ქართულად ვსაუბრობ, უსიამოვნობა ვგრძნობ.
- rodesac kartulad vsaubrobo, usiamovnoba vgrtsnob.
- 'When I speak in Georgian, I feel discomfort.'

Example 2:
- სანამ წიგნს ვკითხულობ, ხმა არ შემომხმარებოდეს.
- sanam ts'igns vk'itkhulob, khma ar shemomkhmarebodes.
- 'While I'm reading the book, don't distract me.'

**Conditional Clauses (If):**

**[Show conditional patterns]**

- თუ (tu) - 'if'
- რომც (romc) - 'even if'

Example 3:
- თუ ბევრს ივარჯიშებ, ქართულ ენას დაეუფლები.
- tu bevrs ivardzisheb, kartul enas daeuplebi.
- 'If you practice a lot, you'll master the Georgian language.'

Example 4:
- რომც რთული იყოს, მაინც გავაკეთებ.
- romc rtuli iq'os, maints gavak'eteb.
- 'Even if it's difficult, I'll still do it.'

**Causal Clauses (Because):**

- იმიტომ რომ (imit'om rom) - 'because'
- რადგან (radgan) - 'because' (more formal)

Example 5:
- მე დავეწყე იმიტომ რომ ქართული კულტურა ჩემთვის საინტერესოა.
- me davets'q'e imit'om rom kartuli k'ult'ura chemt'vis saint'eresoa.
- 'I started because Georgian culture is interesting to me.'

**Purpose Clauses (So that / In order to):**

- რათა (rata) - 'so that / in order to'
- რომ (rom) - can also express purpose

Example 6:
- ქართულად ვსწავლობ რათა ქართველ მეგობრებთან ვისაუბრო.
- kartulad vsts'avlob rata kartvel megobretan visaubro.
- 'I study Georgian so that I can talk with Georgian friends.'

These adverbial clauses add crucial context to our sentences!"

### Main Content Section 3: Coordination (50:00 - 60:00)
**[Show coordinating conjunctions]**

"Coordination links equal clauses using conjunctions. Georgian has several coordinating patterns:

**Common Coordinating Conjunctions:**

- და (da) - 'and'
- მაგრამ (magram) - 'but'
- ან (an) - 'or'
- ასე რომ (ase rom) - 'so'

Example 1:
- მე ვფიქრობ ქართული ლექსი და ისიც ასე ფიქრობს.
- me vp'ik'rob kartuli lek'si da isits ase p'ik'robs.
- 'I think Georgian is beautiful, and he/she also thinks so.'

Example 2:
- რთულია ქართული, მაგრამ საინტერესო.
- rtulia kartuli, magram saint'ereso.
- 'Georgian is difficult, but interesting.'

Example 3:
- დავეწყებ ლექციას ან პრაქტიკას ავირჩევ.
- davets'q'eb lek'sias an p'rakt'ik'as avirchev.
- 'I will start a lecture, or I will choose practice.'

**Parallel Structures:**

Georgian loves parallel structures for emphasis:

Example 4:
- ვცდილობ, ვსწავლობ, და ვპრაქტიკობ ყოველდღე.
- vts'dilob, vsts'avlob, da vp'rakt'ik'ob qoveldghe.
- 'I try, I study, and I practice every day.'

This triple coordination creates rhythm and emphasis."

### Summary (65:00 - 68:00)
**[Review all patterns]**

"Let's recap what we've learned about complex sentence embedding:

**Three Types of Embedding:**
1. **Complement Clauses** - Use რომ to embed clauses as objects/subjects
2. **Adverbial Clauses** - Use conjunctions for time, reason, condition, purpose
3. **Relative Clauses** - Covered in previous lesson

**Key Patterns:**
- Complement: Verb + რომ + full clause
- Time: როდესაც, სანამ
- Condition: თუ, რომც
- Cause: იმიტომ რომ, რადგან
- Purpose: რათა, რომ
- Coordination: და, მაგრამ, ან

**Remember:** You can embed multiple levels deep! That's what separates intermediate from advanced speakers."
