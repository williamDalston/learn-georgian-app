# Lesson C1-7: Advanced Conversation: Debates and Discussions

**Level:** C1
**Duration:** 75 minutes
**Module:** 5 | **Lesson:** 7
**Estimated Hours:** 1.25 hours

## Learning Objectives
By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:
- Participate in extended debates on current events and cultural topics
- Express nuanced opinions with appropriate hedging and qualification
- Use advanced argumentation structures in Georgian
- Navigate cultural sensitivities in political and cultural discussions
- Employ formal debate language and respectful disagreement phrases

## Key Concepts
- Debate structures and formats
- Hedging language (მგონია, შეიძლება, ალბათ)
- Argumentation frameworks
- Cultural taboos and sensitive topics
- Diplomatic disagreement expressions
- Evidence-presenting phrases

From the traditional supra where philosophical conversations flow freely to modern political discourse, Georgians value intellectual exchange and passionate argumentation. Georgian debate culture has several unique characteristics:

### Main Content Section 1: Debate Structures and Phrases (5:00 - 20:00)
**[Show debate structure flowchart]**

"Let's start by learning the fundamental structures for building arguments in Georgian. First, let's look at how to introduce a topic and position:

**Opening Your Position:**

1. **როგორც ვიცი, / როგორც ჩემი აზრით**
   *Rogorts vitsi, / Rogorts chemi azrit*
   "As I know / As far as I'm concerned"
   *Example: როგორც ჩემი აზრით, ეკონომიკური პოლიტიკა სჭირდება განახლებას.*
   *"As far as I'm concerned, economic policy needs updating."*

2. **თუ კარგად დავფიქრდე**
   *Tu k'argad davp'ik'rde*
   "If I think about it carefully"
   *Creates space for considered opinion*

3. **სიმართლე რომ ვთქვათ**
   *Simartle rom vtk'vat*
   "To be truthful / Honestly speaking"

**[Show political debate example on screen]**

Now let's look at building your argument:

**Presenting Evidence:**

1. **ფაქტია, რომ**
   *P'akt'ia, rom*
   "The fact is that..."
   *Factual grounding*

2. **მაგალითად**
   *Magalitad*
   "For example"

3. **შემთხვევების უმეტესობა**
   *Shemtkhvevebis umetesoba*
   "In most cases"
   *Qualifying your evidence*

4. **სტატისტიკური მონაცემები აჩვენებენ**
   *St'at'istik'uri monatsemebi achveneben*
   "Statistical data shows"
   *Academic tone*

'As far as I'm concerned, the education system needs to be strengthened...'"

### Main Content Section 2: Hedging and Qualification (20:00 - 35:00)
**[Display hedging phrases with tonal variation]**

"Georgian culture, while valuing strong opinions, also appreciates intellectual humility. Hedging - softening your statements - is crucial for nuanced debate. Let's examine Georgian hedging strategies:

**Probability Hedges:**

1. **მგონია**
   *Mgonia*
   "I think / It seems to me"
   *Most common hedge*
   
2. **შეიძლება**
   *Sheidzleba*
   "Perhaps / Maybe"
   *Soft uncertainty*

3. **ალბათ**
   *Albat*
   "Probably / Likely"
   *Higher probability*

4. **არ არის გამორიცხული**
   *Ar aris gamoritskhuli*
   "It's not excluded"
   *Diplomatic possibility*

5. **დიდი ალბათობით**
   *Didi albat'obit*
   "Most likely / Very probably"

**Cultural Note:** Georgian intellectuals often use hedging to show humility despite strong opinions.

**[Demonstrate with examples on screen]**

Consider this progression:
- **Strong:** *ეს კატასტროფაა* ("This is a catastrophe")
- **Moderate:** *მგონია, ეს სერიოზული პრობლემაა* ("I think this is a serious problem")
- **Soft:** *შეიძლება, ეს შეიძლება პრობლემა გახდეს* ("Perhaps this might become a problem")

**Practice Exercise 2 (25:00 - 30:00)**
**[Live hedging practice]**

"Rephrase this strong statement with appropriate hedging:
*ეკონომიკა უბრალოდ ჩავარდნილია* ('The economy has simply collapsed')

Good answers include: *მგონია, ეკონომიკამ გართულებების გამო განიცდა ზეგავლენა*
('I think the economy has experienced difficulties due to complications')"

### Main Content Section 3: Disagreement and Counter-Argument (35:00 - 50:00)
**[Cultural context: Georgian respect hierarchies]**

"Disagreement in Georgian requires cultural finesse. Direct contradiction can be perceived as disrespectful, especially with older speakers. Learn the art of polite disagreement:

**Soft Disagreement:**

1. **არ შემიძლია შევთანხმდე 100%**
   *Ar shemidzlia shevtanx'mde 100%*
   "I can't agree 100%"
   *Softest form*

2. **თუმცა, სხვა კუთხითაც შეიძლება შევხედოთ**
   *Tumts'a, skhva kutkhitats sheidzleba shekhedot*
   "However, we can also look at it from another angle"

3. **მოდით, ცოტა უფრო ღრმად განვიხილოთ**
   *Modit, tsot'a upro ghrmad ganvikhilot*
   "Let's examine this a bit more deeply"
   *Intellectual framing*

**Moderate Disagreement:**

1. **გეთანხმებით ნაწილობრივ, მაგრამ...**
   *Getanx'mebit nats'ilobriv, magram...*
   "I partly agree with you, but..."
   *Balanced approach*

2. **ეს ინტერესული პერსპექტივაა, თუმცა...**
   *Es int'eresuli p'erspekt'ivaa, tumts'a...*
   "That's an interesting perspective, however..."

3. **გაქვთ მართალი, თუმცა ასევე გასათვალისწინებელია...**
   *Gakvt martali, tumts'a aseve gasatvalists'inebelia...*
   "You're right, but we should also consider..."

**Stronger Disagreement (use carefully):**

1. **ლოგიკურად, ეს არ იწერება**
   *Logik'urad, es ar its'ereba*
   "Logically, that doesn't follow"
   *Academic tone*

2. **სამწუხაროდ, ამ მონაცემებს არ ვიზიარებ**
   *Samts'ukharod, am monatsemebs ar viziareb*
   "Unfortunately, I don't share this data"

**[Show debate scenario with proper disagreement escalation]**

**Cultural Taboo Alert:** Never directly contradict Georgian history, Orthodox Christianity, or family values in public debate. Focus on policy alternatives, not core values.

### Main Content Section 4: Current Events Vocabulary (50:00 - 65:00)
**[Build topic-specific vocabulary]**

"For successful debate, you need topical vocabulary. Let's build essential words for political and social discourse:

**Government & Politics:**
- **მთავრობა** *Mtavroba* - Government
- **პარლამენტი** *Parlament'i* - Parliament
- **მთავარი მინისტრი** *Mtavari ministri* - Prime Minister
- **არჩევნები** *Archevnebi* - Elections
- **დემოკრატია** *Demok'rat'ia* - Democracy
- **კორუფცია** *K'oruptsia* - Corruption

**Economy:**
- **ეკონომიკა** *Ek'onomik'a* - Economy
- **ინფლაცია** *Inplatsia* - Inflation
- **უმუშევრობა** *Umush'evroba* - Unemployment
- **ბიუჯეტი** *Biujet'i* - Budget
- **საბიუჯეტო დეფიციტი** *Sabiujet'o depitsit'i* - Budget deficit

**Social Issues:**
- **სოციალური სამართლიანობა** *Sotsialuri samartlianoba* - Social justice
- **იმიგრაცია** *Imigratsia* - Immigration
- **გლობალიზაცია** *Globalizatsia* - Globalization
- **გარემო** *Garemo* - Environment

### Summary (72:00 - 75:00)
**[Key takeaways on screen]**

"Let's review what we've learned today:

1. **Debate Openers**: Use *როგორც ჩემი აზრით*, *როგორც ვიცი* to frame positions
2. **Evidence**: Ground arguments with *ფაქტია რომ*, statistics, examples
3. **Hedging**: Soften with *მგონია*, *შეიძლება*, *ალბათ*
4. **Disagreement**: Respect hierarchy with *არ შემიძლია შევთანხმდე*, *თუმცა...*
5. **Cultural Sensitivity**: Avoid direct contradiction on core values
6. **Vocabulary**: Master topical terms for politics, economy, society

Remember: Georgian debate values passionate expression within respectful cultural boundaries."
