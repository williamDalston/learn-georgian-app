# Lesson B1-3: Version Vowels: Subjective (ი-) and Objective (უ-)

**Level:** B1
**Duration:** 70 minutes
**Module:** 3 | **Lesson:** 3
**Estimated Hours:** 1.17 hours

## Learning Objectives
By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:
- Understand what version vowels are in Georgian
- Distinguish between subjective (ი-) and objective (უ-) versions
- Use version vowels to express different perspectives and relationships
- Recognize version vowels in verb conjugations
- Apply version vowels to construct nuanced sentences
- Identify version vs non-version verb forms

## Key Concepts
- Version vowels (subjective vs objective)
- ი- [i-] version (subjective, for oneself)
- უ- [u-] version (objective, for others)
- Version + preverb combinations
- Version's role in verb meaning

## Video Script

### Opening (0:00 - 0:30)
**[Instructor with enthusiasm]**

"Gamarjoba! Welcome back. You've mastered polypersonal verbs and preverbs - fantastic progress! Today, we're exploring another beautiful feature of Georgian: version vowels. These tiny vowels can completely change a verb's meaning, showing whose benefit or perspective the action is for. This is truly unique to Georgian, and it's going to make your speech so much more nuanced!"

---

### Introduction (0:30 - 5:00)
**[Show comparison: same verb with different versions]**

"Look at these three Georgian sentences using the same verb:

1. ვწერ წიგნს [vts'er ts'igns] - 'I am writing the book'
2. ვიწერ წიგნს [vits'er ts'igns] - 'I am writing the book for myself'
3. ვუწერ წიგნს [vuts'er ts'igns] - 'I am writing the book for someone else'

What's the difference? Just a tiny vowel: ი- [i-] or უ- [u-] after the subject marker!

**[Display visual explanation]**

These vowels are called 'version vowels' - they indicate whose benefit or perspective the action is for:
- **ი- [i-]** = subjective version (for myself, my own benefit)
- **უ- [u-]** = objective version (for others, someone else's benefit)
- **No version** = neutral/standard action

This is like adding a layer of perspective to every verb!"

---

### Main Content Section 1: Subjective Version ი- [i-] (5:00 - 25:00)
**[Display subjective version explanation]**

"The subjective version, marked by ი- [i-], means the action is for the subject's own benefit, interest, or perspective.

Think of it as: 'for myself,' 'to my own,' 'for my benefit'

**[Show examples]**

Example 1: Writing
- ვწერ წიგნს [vts'er ts'igns] - 'I am writing the book' (neutral)
- ვიწერ წიგნს [vits'er ts'igns] - 'I am writing the book for myself' (subjective)

Example 2: Building
- ვაშენებ სახლს [vasheneb sakhls] - 'I am building a house' (neutral)
- ვიაშენებ სახლს [viasheneb sakhls] - 'I am building a house for myself' (subjective)

Example 3: Buying
- ვყიდულობ პურის [vqidulob p'uris] - 'I am buying bread' (neutral)
- ვიყიდულობ პურის [viqidulob p'uris] - 'I am buying bread for myself' (subjective)

**[Show conjugation]**

Let's conjugate with subjective version. Here's წერა [ts'era] 'to write' with ი- [i-]:

**Subjective Version Conjugation (ი- [i-]):**
- მე ვიწერ წიგნს [me vits'er ts'igns] - I write for myself
- შენ იწერ წიგნს [shen its'er ts'igns] - You write for yourself
- ის იწერს წიგნს [is its'ers ts'igns] - He/She writes for himself/herself
- ჩვენ ვიწერთ წიგნს [ch'ven vits'ert ts'igns] - We write for ourselves
- თქვენ იწერთ წიგნს [tkven its'ert ts'igns] - You write for yourselves
- ისინი იწერენ წიგნს [isini its'eren ts'igns] - They write for themselves

**[Highlight pattern]**

Notice the pattern:
- ი- [i-] comes after the subject marker
- პირველ პირი [p'irveli p'iri] 'first person': ვიწერ [vits'er]
- მესამე პირი [mesame p'iri] 'third person': იწერს [its'ers]"

---

### Main Content Section 2: Objective Version უ- [u-] (25:00 - 45:00)
**[Display objective version explanation]**

"The objective version, marked by უ- [u-], means the action is for someone else's benefit, directed toward another person.

Think of it as: 'for you/him/her/them,' 'to others,' 'for their benefit'

**[Show examples]**

Example 1: Writing
- ვწერ წიგნს [vts'er ts'igns] - 'I am writing the book' (neutral)
- ვუწერ წიგნს [vuts'er ts'igns] - 'I am writing the book for someone' (objective)

Example 2: Cooking
- ვხარშავ ლაღს [vkharshav laghs] - 'I am cooking food' (neutral)
- ვუხარშავ ლაღს [vukharshav laghs] - 'I am cooking food for someone' (objective)

Example 3: Buying
- ვყიდულობ წიგნს [vqidulob ts'igns] - 'I am buying a book' (neutral)
- ვუყიდულობ წიგნს [vuqidulob ts'igns] - 'I am buying a book for someone' (objective)

**[Show conjugation]**

Now let's conjugate with objective version. Here's წერა [ts'era] with უ- [u-]:

**Objective Version Conjugation (უ- [u-]):**
- მე ვუწერ წიგნს [me vuts'er ts'igns] - I write for someone
- შენ უწერ წიგნს [shen uts'er ts'igns] - You write for someone
- ის უწერს წიგნს [is uts'ers ts'igns] - He/She writes for someone
- ჩვენ ვუწერთ წიგნს [ch'ven vuts'ert ts'igns] - We write for someone
- თქვენ უწერთ წიგნს [tkven uts'ert ts'igns] - You write for someone
- ისინი უწერენ წიგნს [isini uts'eren ts'igns] - They write for someone

**[Highlight pattern]**

უ- [u-] also comes after the subject marker:
- პირველი პირი [p'irveli p'iri]: ვუწერ [vuts'er]
- მესამე პირი [mesame p'iri]: უწერს [uts'ers]"

### Main Content Section 3: Version + Preverb Combinations (45:00 - 60:00)
**[Display combination rules]**

"Here's where it gets exciting! Version vowels can combine with preverbs. The order is:
**Subject marker + Preverb + Version vowel + Verb root**

**[Show formula]**
Structure: ვ- + და- + ი- + წერ = ვდაიწერ [vdaints'er]

Example 1: Perfective + Subjective
- Base: დავწერ [davts'er] - 'I will write'
- With subjective: ვდაიწერ [vdaints'er] - 'I will write for myself'

Example 2: Perfective + Objective
- Base: დავწერ [davts'er] - 'I will write'
- With objective: ვდაუწერ [vdauts'er] - 'I will write for someone'

**[Show more examples]**

Example 3: Direction + Version
- Base: მივხედავ [mivkhedav] - 'I will look toward'
- With subjective: მივხედავ შენკენ [mivkhedav shenken] - 'I will look toward you' (general)
- More specific: Notice versions often combine meaning!

**[Interactive practice]**

Let's practice identifying versions:

Question 1: ვიკითხულობ წიგნს [vik'ithulob ts'igns] - Which version?
Answer: სუბიექტური ვერსია [subiekt'uri versia] - Subjective! (ვი- [vi-] = for myself)

Question 2: ვუკითხულობ წიგნს [vuk'ithulob ts'igns] - Which version?
Answer: ობიექტური ვერსია [obiekt'uri versia] - Objective! (ვუ- [vu-] = for someone)

Question 3: ვკითხულობ წიგნს [vk'ithulob ts'igns] - Which version?
Answer: ნეიტრალური [neit'raluri] - Neutral! (No version vowel)

Great job! You're mastering this!"
