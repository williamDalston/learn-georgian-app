# Lesson A2-3: Postpositions and Case Requirements

**Level:** A2
**Duration:** 60 minutes
**Module:** 2 | **Lesson:** 3
**Estimated Hours:** 1 hour

## Learning Objectives
By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:
- Understand what postpositions are and how they differ from prepositions
- Master common postpositions (-tvis, -shi, -dan) and their case requirements
- Apply the correct case endings when using postpositions
- Build sentences using postpositions with proper case agreement

## Key Concepts
- Postpositions vs prepositions
- Postposition-case pairing
- Common postpositions: -tvis, -shi, -dan
- Noun → Case → Postposition pattern

---

## Video Script

### Opening (0:00 - 0:30)
"Gamarjoba! We've mastered cases and plurals. Today, we're learning about postpositions - a crucial feature of Georgian grammar. Unlike English prepositions (like 'for', 'in', 'from') that come before nouns, Georgian postpositions come after nouns and require specific cases. This is one of the most important concepts for building correct Georgian sentences!"

---

### Introduction (0:30 - 5:00)
**[Show comparison: English prepositions vs Georgian postpositions]**

"In English, we say 'for the child' - the preposition 'for' comes BEFORE the noun.

In Georgian, we say 'ბავშვისთვის' (bavshvistvis - for the child) - the postposition 'თვის' (tvis) comes AFTER the noun.

Notice something important? The noun is in Genitive case: ბავშვის (bavshvis), then we add -თვის (-tvis).

This is the pattern: **Noun (in required case) + Postposition**

Each postposition requires a specific case. Let's learn the most common ones!"

---

### Main Content Section 1: -tvis (for, to) (5:00 - 20:00)

**Postposition: -tvis (for, to)**

"The postposition -tvis means 'for' or 'to' and requires Genitive case.

**[Show pattern]**
Noun (Genitive) + -tvis = 'for the noun'

**[Show examples]**
- ბავშვის (child's - Genitive) + -თვის = ბავშვისთვის (bavshvistvis) - for the child
- წიგნის (book's - Genitive) + -თვის = წიგნისთვის (ts'ignistvis) - for the book
- სახლის (house's - Genitive) + -თვის = სახლისთვის (sakhlistvis) - for the house

**[Show sentence examples]**
ეს არის ბავშვისთვის [es aris bavshvistvis] - This is for the child

მე ვყიდი წიგნს წიგნისთვის [me vq'idi ts'igns ts'ignistvis] - I'm buying a book for the book (I'm buying a book to add to my book collection)

Wait, that second example is a bit unusual - let me give you a better one:

მე ვყიდი საჩუქარს ბავშვისთვის [me vq'idi sachuk'ars bavshvistvis] - I'm buying a gift for the child

**[Key point]**
Remember: Genitive case + -tvis. The Genitive ending -ის becomes part of the noun before adding -თვის."

---

### Main Content Section 2: -shi (in, at) (20:00 - 35:00)

**Postposition: -shi (in, at)**

"The postposition -shi means 'in' or 'at' and requires Dative case.

**[Show pattern]**
Noun (Dative) + -shi = 'in/at the noun'

**[Show examples]**
- სახლში (sakhlshi) - in the house
  - Base: სახლი (sakhli - Nominative)
  - Dative: სახლს (sakhls)
  - Add -shi: სახლში (sakhl-shi)

- წიგნში (ts'ignshi) - in the book
  - Base: წიგნი (ts'igni)
  - Dative: წიგნს (ts'igns)
  - Add -shi: წიგნში (ts'ign-shi)

- კლასში (klas'shi) - in the class
  - Base: კლასი (klasi)
  - Dative: კლასს (klass)
  - Add -shi: კლასში (klas-shi)

**[Show sentence examples]**
წიგნი სახლშია [ts'igni sakhlshia] - The book is in the house

მე ვარ კლასში [me var klas'shi] - I am in class

ბავშვი თამაშობს სახლში [bavshvi tamashobs sakhlshi] - The child plays in the house

**[Key point]**
Dative case ending -ს + -shi. Notice how -ს- and -sh- combine."

---

### Main Content Section 3: -dan (from, since) (35:00 - 50:00)

**Postposition: -dan (from, since)**

"The postposition -dan means 'from' or 'since' and requires Genitive case.

**[Show pattern]**
Noun (Genitive) + -dan = 'from the noun'

**[Show examples]**
- სახლიდან (sakhlidan) - from the house
  - Base: სახლი (sakhli)
  - Genitive: სახლის (sakhlis)
  - Add -dan: სახლიდან (sakhl-is-dan)

- წიგნიდან (ts'ignidan) - from the book
  - Base: წიგნი (ts'igni)
  - Genitive: წიგნის (ts'ignis)
  - Add -dan: წიგნიდან (ts'ign-is-dan)

- ქალაქიდან (kalak'idan) - from the city
  - Base: ქალაქი (kalak'i)
  - Genitive: ქალაქის (kalak'is)
  - Add -dan: ქალაქიდან (kalak'-is-dan)

**[Show sentence examples]**
მე მოვდივარ სახლიდან [me movdivar sakhlidan] - I am coming from the house

ეს არის ქალაქიდან [es aris kalak'idan] - This is from the city

**[Key point]**
Genitive case ending -ის + -dan. The -ის becomes part of the noun before -dan."

### Summary (57:00 - 60:00)
"Let's review postpositions:

1. **-tvis** (for, to) - Requires Genitive case
   - Example: ბავშვისთვის (for the child)

2. **-shi** (in, at) - Requires Dative case
   - Example: სახლში (in the house)

3. **-dan** (from, since) - Requires Genitive case
   - Example: სახლიდან (from the house)

**The Pattern:** Noun (in required case) + Postposition

This is crucial: each postposition requires a specific case. Learning which case goes with which postposition is essential for correct Georgian!

In the next lesson, we'll start building complete sentences with SVO structure."
